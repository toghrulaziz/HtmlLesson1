The east coast may have the dawn, but here in San Juan, La Union, we get the sunsets. Cumulus clouds pile high over the horizon as waves obliterate themselves on the sand, turning into a mist, making everything glow. Along the shoreline, giant orbs made from bamboo, recycled plastic, and steel rise into the sky, like a spaceship ready for takeoff. Created by Filipino artist Leeroy New, Mebuyan’s Vessel is an outdoor artwork originally commissioned for the 2020 Burning Man festival. Because of the pandemic, it touched down here, instead.
—Nicola Sebastian, Produced by National Geographic Creativeworks
